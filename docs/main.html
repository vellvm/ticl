Up-to-tau equivalence (strong bisimulation)

A core result of Ticl is that Ticl formulas are invariant under up-to-tau equivalence of ICTrees. We define up-to-tau equivalence (which we call strong bisimulation in the Rocq development) in the following definitions (Figure 5).

  • Up-to-tau equivalence: Definition of strong bisimulation (up-to-tau equivalence in the paper) for ICTree.
  • sb_refl: Reflexivity of sbisim.
  • sb_sym: Symmetry of sbisim.
  • sb_trans: Transitivity of sbisim.
  • sb_tau: Up-to-tau (we use Guard instead of Tau in the Rocq development).
  • sb_bind: Bind closure.
  • sb_bind_l: 1st monad law (for coinductive equality, a subrelation of sbisim).
  • sb_bind_r: 2nd monad law (for coinductive equality, a subrelation of sbisim).
  • sb_bind_assoc: Associativity of bind (for coinductive equality, a subrelation of sbisim).
  • sb_ret: Ret node congruence.
  • sb_vis: Vis node congruence.
  • sb_br: Br node congruence (we prove a stronger version in the Rocq development than in the paper).
  • KripkeSetoidSBisim: This is the ExEquiv lemma that is used to prove that Ticl formulas are invariant under up-to-tau equivalence of ICTrees (Lemma 3).
  • Kripke setoids preserve entailment: Up-to-tau equivalence preserves prefix formulas theorem (Theorem 4).
  • Kripke setoids preserve entailment: Up-to-tau equivalence preserves suffix formulas theorem (Theorem 4).

Ticl implications and equivalences

The following define the equational theory and implications of Ticl formulas (Definition 2, Figure 13). These definitions are motivated in Section 4.5 of the paper.

  • impl_ticll: Definition of prefix implication for Ticl.
  • impl_ticlr: Definition of suffix implication for Ticl.
  • equiv_ticll: Definition of prefix equivalence for Ticl.
  • equiv_ticlr: Definition of suffix equivalence for Ticl.
  • ticll_an_en: AN is stronger than EN (prefix).
  • ticll_au_eu: AU is stronger than EU (prefix).
  • ticll_ag_eg: AG is stronger than EG.
  • ticlr_an_en: AN is stronger than EN (suffix).
  • ticlr_au_eu: AU is stronger than EU (suffix).
  • ticll_an_au: AN is stronger than AU (prefix).
  • ticll_en_eu: EN is stronger than EU (prefix).
  • ticlr_an_au: AN is stronger than AU (suffix).
  • ticlr_en_eu: EN is stronger than EU (suffix).
  • impll_ag_refl: Reflexivity of AG.
  • impll_eg_refl: Reflexivity of EG.
  • impll_or_ag: AG commutes with OR.
  • impll_or_eg: EG commutes with OR.
  • equivl_and_ag: AG commutes with AND.
  • equivl_au_an: Unfolding AU into AN (prefix).
  • equivl_eu_en: Unfolding EU into EN (prefix).
  • equivl_ag_an: Unfolding AG into AN.
  • equivl_eg_en: Unfolding EG into EN.
  • equivr_au_an: Unfolding AU into AN (suffix).
  • equivr_eu_en: Unfolding EU into EN (suffix).
  • equivl_au_idem: AU is idempotent (prefix).
  • equivl_eu_idem: EU is idempotent (prefix).
  • equivl_ag_idem: AG is idempotent.
  • equivl_eg_idem: EG is idempotent.
  • equivr_au_idem: AU is idempotent (suffix).
  • equivr_eu_idem: EU is idempotent (suffix).
